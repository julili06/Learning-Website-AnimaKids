Bats, Bears, and Beyond

Fur, Fangs, and Fluff: A Journey into Mammalian Diversity

Mammals are animals that have hair or fur on their bodies. They also feed their babies with milk from their mothers.
This means animals like cats, dogs, elephants, and even humans are mammals! Mammals can be found all over the world, from the smallest mouse to the biggest whale.


Fun Facts and More about Mammals

Diversity and Orders

Over 6,400 extant species of mammals exist, divided into 29 orders.

Warm-Blooded Wonders

All mammals have warm blood. That means they can warm up and cool down their bodies. Imagine a polar bear living in the snow with the same body temperature as a monkey swinging through the jungle!

Moving Marvels

Mammals move in different ways: Some trot on four legs.Horses and big cats can run super fast. Bats are the only mammals that can fly. Moles have feet like shovels for burrowing. Dolphins and whales use flippers and tails to swim.

Backbone Basics

Mammals have backbones. No matter how different we look, we all have bony skeletons that hold us up and keep us going. Animals with backbones are called vertebrates.

Furry Fashion

Mammals have hair or fur. Some fur is short, some is long, and some even comes in cool patterns. But we all rock our natural coats!
