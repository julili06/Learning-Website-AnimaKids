Reptiles

What about our Diverse Dinosaurs?

Exploring the World of Scales and Adaptations

Reptiles are animals that have scaly skin and lay eggs. They are cold-blooded, which means they need to bask in the sun to warm up.
Reptiles include snakes, turtles, crocodiles, and lizards. They're amazing creatures that have been around for millions of years!


Fun Facts and More about Reptiles

Long-Lived Creatures

Reptiles are some of the longest-living species on Earth. For example: The Aldabra tortoise can live over 150 years. Alligators have an average life span of seventy years.

Live Births

While most reptiles lay eggs, some snakes, like the boa constrictor, give live births.

Diverse Species

There are more than 8,200 different species of reptiles. Most of these are snakes and lizards.

Survival Skills

Reptiles can go months without eating. Talk about patience!

Giant Crocs

The world’s largest reptile is the saltwater crocodile, measuring over 20 feet long!

Tiny Chameleon

The world’s smallest reptile is the Brookesia nana, or nano-chameleon. It’s only 13.5 mm long – quite the little guy!

Did you know?

Proto-reptiles emerged during the Carboniferous period from advanced tetrapods.The earliest true reptile was Hylonomus, resembling a small lizard, around 318 million years ago.
