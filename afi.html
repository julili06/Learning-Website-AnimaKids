Fish

Fins, Scales, and Survival

Beyond Scales: A Deep Dive into Aquatic Biodiversity

Fish are animals that live in water and breathe through their gills. They have scales covering their bodies and fins to help them swim. Fish come in all colors and sizes, from tiny guppies to huge sharks! They're super cool creatures that glide through the water, exploring the oceans, rivers, and lakes.


Fun Facts and More about Fish

Diverse Fishy Friends

Fish come in all shapes, sizes, and colors. From the tiny neon tetra to the giant whale shark, there's a fish for every aquatic adventure!

Underwater Acrobats

Some fish can jump out of the water and glide through the air! It's like they have their own superpower.

Colorful Crew

Many fish are brightly colored to attract mates or blend in with their surroundings. Just like wearing your favorite outfit, fish use colors to express themselves!

Scale Tales

Fish are covered in scales that act like armor, protecting them from predators. Some fish even have special scales that shimmer and shine in the sunlight.

Bubble Builders

Betta fish are famous for their bubble nests, which they build on the surface of the water. It's like their own underwater castle!

School's in Session

Some fish swim in groups called schools. They stick together to stay safe and find food, just like friends hanging out together.

Did you know?

Fish evolved during the Cambrian period and continued diversifying through the Paleozoic era. The first fish with jaws (placoderms) appeared in the Silurian, leading to the “Age of Fishes” during the Devonian period.
