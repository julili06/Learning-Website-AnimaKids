The Animal Kingdom

Animals are eukaryotic, multicellular, species belonging to the Kingdom Animalia. Every animal has its own unique characteristics. They obtain their energy either by feeding on plants or on other animals. There are millions of species which have been identified, few share similar characteristics while others differ drastically.

Animal Classification

Animals are classified based on their characteristics. They are eminent from algae, plants, and fungus where rigid cell walls are absent. Some are also heterotrophic, in general, they digest their food within the internal chambers which again distinguish them from algae and plants. Another elite character of these species is that they are motile, except in certain life stages.



Basic Types of Animals and their Characteristics

The Animal Kingdom
All animals belong to a biological kingdom called kingdom Animalia. This kingdom is then broken down into over 30 groups, or phyla (plural form of phylum). About 75% of all species on Earth are animals. Animals are then broken down into two types: vertebrates and invertebrates.



More about Amphibians

All amphibians are vertebrates, and they need moist environments or water to survive. You can find examples of amphibians all around the world. In some cases, examples of amphibians seem like the same animals, but they can be quite different like a frog and a toad

Adaptations and Life Cycle

Amphibians start as aquatic larvae (tadpoles) with gills, but many species have adapted to bypass this stage. They undergo metamorphosis from aquatic larvae to air-breathing adults with lungs.

Habitats and Decline

Most amphibians inhabit freshwater, wetlands, or terrestrial ecosystems. They are ecological indicators, reflecting habitat conditions.

anamniotic

They are anamniotic, lacking an amniotic membrane like reptiles, birds, and mammals.

More about Birds

Birds come in many colors and sizes, from tiny hummingbirds to big eagles. They sing beautiful songs and build nests to raise their babies. Birds are amazing creatures that can be found all around the world!

High Flyers

Some birds, like the Arctic tern, migrate incredible distances every year, traveling from the Arctic to the Antarctic and back again. That's like flying from one end of the Earth to the other!

Parenting Partners

Many birds work together to raise their chicks, taking turns keeping them warm and bringing them food. It's a real family affair in the bird world!

Lifelong Companions

Some birds, like bald eagles, swans, and doves, live together as couples for their entire lives.

More about Reptiles

The term “reptile” is derived from a Latin word meaning “creeping animals.” Reptiles are thought to be the first vertebrates to live completely on land. But, not all reptiles live only on land today.

Evolution

The first reptiles evolved approximately 320 million years ago from the advanced four-limbed vertebrates known as reptiliomorph.

Reptiles Are Oviparous

Most reptiles reproduce sexually while others are capable of reproducing asexually. The reproduction activities take place through the cloaca located at the base of the tail.

Four legged Creeps!

Reptiles have either four legs, or some like snakes, are descendants of four-limbed ancestors. In most snakes, all traces of legs including bones for the legs have disappeared.

More about Mammals

A mammal is a fascinating vertebrate animal belonging to the class Mammalia.Mammals are distinguished by several other unique features. Hair is a typical mammalian feature, although in many whales it has disappeared except in the fetal stage. The mammalian lower jaw is hinged directly to the skull, instead of through a separate bone (the quadrate) as in all other vertebrates.

Diversity

The evolution of the class Mammalia has produced tremendous diversity in form and habit. Living kinds range in size from a bat weighing less than a gram and tiny shrews weighing but a few grams to the largest animal that has ever lived, the blue whale, which reaches a length of more than 30 metres (100 feet) and a weight of 180 metric tons (nearly 200 short [U.S.] tons).

Intelligence and Communication

Mammals exhibit intelligence, self-awareness, and tool use. They communicate through ultrasound, scent-marking, vocalizations, and even complex language (in humans).

Implantation, gestation, and birth

Reproductive patterns in placental mammals are diverse, but in all cases a secretory phase is present in the uterine cycle, and the endometrium is maintained by secretions of progesterone from the corpus luteum.

More about Invertebrates

More than 90 percent of all living animal species are invertebrates. Worldwide in distribution, they include animals as diverse as sea stars, sea urchins, earthworms, sponges, jellyfish, lobsters, crabs, insects, spiders, snails, clams, and squid. Invertebrates are especially important as agricultural pests, parasites, or agents for the transmission of parasitic infections to humans and other vertebrates.

Diverse Group

Invertebrates make up about 97% of all animal species on Earth¹. This group includes insects, arachnids, mollusks, crustaceans, and more.

Regeneration

Some invertebrates, like starfish, can regenerate lost body parts. A starfish can regrow an entire arm if it gets cut off.

Unique Structures

Octopuses have three hearts and blue blood. Two hearts pump blood to the gills, while the third pumps it to the rest of the body.

More about Fish

The term fish is applied to a variety of vertebrates of several evolutionary lines. It describes a life-form rather than a taxonomic group.Fish have developed special senses, too. Because water transmits sounds, disperses chemicals, and conducts electricity better than air, fish rely less on their vision and more on their hearing, taste, and smell.

Fish Schools

Fish often swim in schools for protection and to improve their chances of finding food. Some schools can contain millions of fish

Colorful Defenders

Many fish use bright colors to camouflage themselves or to warn predators of their toxicity. For example, the lionfish has venomous spines and a striking appearance

Unique Breathers

The lungfish can survive out of water for several years by burrowing into mud and breathing air through a lung-like structure2
