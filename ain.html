Invertebrates

Creepy Crawlers' Wild world

Spineless Marvels: From Jellyfish to Centipedes

Invertebrates are animals without a backbone. That means they don't have bones like we do. Instead, they have soft bodies or hard shells to protect them. There are lots of different types of invertebrates, like insects, spiders, snails, and jellyfish. They come in all shapes and sizes and can be found everywhere, from your backyard to the deepest parts of the ocean!


Fun Facts and More about Invertebrates

Fantastic Insects

Did you know that insects are the largest group of invertebrates? From buzzing bees to beautiful butterflies, insects are everywhere, and they come in all shapes and sizes!

Marvelous Mollusks

Some invertebrates, like snails and octopuses, belong to a group called mollusks. They have soft bodies and come in a variety of cool shapes, like spirals and tentacles.

Sensational Spiders

Spiders are amazing architects, spinning intricate webs to catch their prey. They may seem spooky, but they're actually helpful in keeping insect populations in check.

Rockstar Crustaceans

Crabs, lobsters, and shrimp are part of a group called crustaceans. They have hard shells to protect their bodies and can be found in oceans, rivers, and even on land!

Jellyfish Jive

Jellyfish are some of the most mesmerizing creatures in the ocean. With their graceful movements and translucent bodies, they're like living works of art.

Incredible Adaptations

Invertebrates showcase incredible adaptations to survive in their environments. From camouflage to unique body structures, these creatures have fascinating ways to thrive in the wild.

Did you know?

The term “invertebrate” comes from the Latin word “vertebra,” meaning a joint, and the prefix “in-” means “not” or “without”. Over 90% of all species on Earth are invertebrates. They’ve been found in the fossil record dating back 600 million years.
